💡 **What**: Refactored `AutonomousBusinessOrchestrator` to use an O(1) `task_map` (Dict[str, AutonomousTask]) and an `in_progress_tasks` Set[str], avoiding O(N) list comprehensions over the unbounded `task_queue` in `_reconcile_orphaned_in_progress_tasks` and `_execute_tasks_parallel`.

🎯 **Why**: As the `task_queue` grows over the lifetime of a business, iterating through it on every run loop iteration (every 5 seconds via CEO daemon) to find the tiny subset of `IN_PROGRESS` tasks causes measurable and unnecessary CPU spikes, risking event loop blocking.

📊 **Impact**: Reduces time complexity for fetching active tasks from O(N) (where N is total historical tasks) to O(K) (where K is the number of currently active agents, usually <10). Prevents performance degradation over time as historical tasks accumulate.

🔬 **Measurement**: Verified via `test_autonomous_orchestrator.py`. Can be monitored in production by observing lower CPU utilization metrics during the business orchestration loop for long-running businesses.
